<p id="tribute-info">
Maya Angelou, born Marguerite Annie Johnson on **April 4, 1928**, in St. Louis, Missouri, overcame immense challenges to become an influential figure. Here's a glimpse into her remarkable journey<br><br>

Early Life and Trauma:<br><br>
Angelou spent much of her childhood in rural Stamps, Arkansas, under the care of her paternal grandmother. At the age of eight, she experienced a traumatic event: she was raped by her mother's boyfriend. After revealing the incident, he was murdered.
The trauma left her almost completely mute for several years<br><br>

Autobiographical Works<br><br>
Her first autobiographical work, "I Know Why the Caged Bird Sings"(1969), gained critical acclaim and a National Book Award nomination. It covers her life up to age 17.
Subsequent volumes include "Gather Together in My Name," "Singin’ and Swingin’ and Gettin’ Merry Like Christmas," "The Heart of a Woman," and more<br><br>

Career and Achievements<br><br>
Angelou's diverse career spanned singing, dancing, journalism, civil rights activism, and writing.
She received numerous accolades, including the Presidential Medal of Freedom (2011) and multiple Grammy Awards for her spoken word albums

Maya Angelou's resilience and powerful voice continue to inspire generations.</p>
